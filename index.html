Intro

Hello, my name is Florebencia Fils-Aime. I am currentlly a Computer Engineering student aiming for my BA. I am passionate about social justice, supporting more women in STEM and inclusion. In my free time, I like to draw pictures and write poetry. Here's a fun fact about me: I used to have 6 fingers on each hand but it was cut off after I was born. I still have the scar on my hand to this day. By the way, check out my awesome work.

Work

Projects:

  • Mobile Game Application- I am in the process of achieving a mobile game app that is a third person shooter game.
  • Fractal Image Generator - I developed a Java application to display a fractal image recording user inputs.
  • Candy Crush-like Game - I developed a Java game application that allowed the user to switch tiles to create a match.
  • Self-Love Calendar - I developed an interactive java application that used user input to select a day in the calendar, and displayed an inspiring quote based on the day.

Hackathons:

  • WicHacks 2017 at RIT - I led a group of four to develop an educational web application for children.
  • GDI Hacks at Buffalo, NY in 2016: I worked with a team to develop an interactive website for a company to increase customer base.

Work Experience:

  • Residental Hall Association in University at Buffalo- I am a senator. I develop programs to aid in the transition of residents in the halls.
  • Project Reach in Manhattan, NY- I was summer staff. I helped to teach many other organizations about discrimination and how being bigoted can affect their employees and customers.

About

I was born on January 23, 1998 in Montreal, Canada. I was raised in by Haitian parents for my whole life and was raised in both Brooklyn, NY and Queens, NY. I started coding when I was a freshman in University. I started coding because I was interested in Computer Science and I wanted to be a model for black women who want a career in STEM.

Throughout most of my life, I have always been a good student. In middle school, I rose to the top of my class and became the validorian in 2012. With my GPA, I was able to get into the honorable Townsend Harris High School (in Flushing, NY) of which I was able to take honors classes from 2012 to 2016. Within those years, I was able to become the Vice President of my school's Gender-Sexuality Alliance, be a part of yearly school performances and Arista (which is a National Honors Society), and gain the Thomas P. Sweetin Award for Promotion of Human Values and Diversity.

Now I am a sophmore working on my Bachelor's Degree in Computer Engineering, and possibly hoping to get my Masters one day. I am currently in the clubs: UB Association for Computing Machinery (ACM), UB Scientista, National Society of Black Engineers, and Filipino American Student Association.
